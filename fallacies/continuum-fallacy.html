    Continuum Fallacy

    Definition: Dismisses claims because a line or distinction can't be clearly defined.

    Example: You can’t define when a heap becomes a heap, so there’s no such thing as a heap.

    Tip: Vagueness doesn’t make distinctions meaningless.
